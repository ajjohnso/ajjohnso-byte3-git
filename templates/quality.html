Quality: About This Data Set

I had high ambitions about the data I collected, but after cleaning just a couple of the many tables I have collected, I realized how challenging it can be to group and bin the data, especially with missing data and data that may not be completely accurate.

Is the data complete?

For the most part, I was able to collect a good amount of data for each of the days in the study period, however I did run into issues of missing data when my phone died during the middle of the day. When this did happen, it was always at around 3pm during the week. You can see the dip in data on the " "Activities Throughout the Day" graph." The missing data could potentially introduce bias in the data and analysis, but I was able to account for some of the missing data since my schedule is generally consistent during the week.

Below is a graph that looks at data collection for one particular day in the study. In a perfect world, the data collected each hour would be exactly the same, however due to network issues or my phone running out of battery, that is not the case. On this particular day, I wasn't able to collect any day at around 7am.

Is the data coherent?

The data is coherent in the sense that most of the data points occur near my house and around CMU's campus. These are the two places I spent a majority of my time during the study. Additionally, the data variables and their values made sense for what they were measuring.

Is the data correct?

After initially looking at the map, I did not recognize some of the places on the map. Some of the location data were a few buildings or streets off. This was especially true around my house. To fix this, I looked at the different addresses generated by the location activity table and binned the addresses by the general activity during that time. In general, I was surprised at how accurate the data was. All of the places on the map were places I had visited over the past few weeks.

Visualization Design Choices

I decided to use the bar graph with counts of all the data collected on a given day to give the reader a sense of how much data had been collected over the past few weeks. I chose to use a grid visualzation that incorporated day and time, but also type of activity, this way, I was able to incorporate more than two different features. I included a map marking the places I had visited, since that is a great way to visualize location data.
